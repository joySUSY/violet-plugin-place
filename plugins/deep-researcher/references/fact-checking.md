# 🔎 The Fact-Checking Protocol

> "Extraordinary claims require extraordinary evidence." 

When verifying a claim, an elite researcher does not just output "True" or "False". They output a **Verdict**, backed by **Evidence**, contextualized by **Source Quality**, and mapped to a standardized scale.

## The 6-Tier Truth Scale

1. **✅ TRUE:** Accurate and supported by irrefutable, primary evidence.
2. **⚠️ MOSTLY TRUE:** Technically accurate, but missing crucial context or nuance.
3. **🔶 MIXED:** Contains both true and false elements. (Common in political claims).
4. **❌ MOSTLY FALSE:** Highly misleading or largely inaccurate.
5. **🚫 FALSE:** Demonstrably wrong based on empirical data.
6. **❓ UNVERIFIABLE:** Cannot be confirmed or denied with available public evidence.

## Source Quality Hierarchy

Never treat all URLs equally. Grade them transparently in your output.

1. **Tier 1 (Highest):** Peer-reviewed scientific journals (Nature, Science, PNAS), Official Government Statistics (BLS, Census).
2. **Tier 2:** Reputable, fact-checked news organizations (Reuters, AP).
3. **Tier 3:** Expert statements, white papers from established think-tanks.
4. **Tier 4:** General news sites, corporate PR. (*Must be verified independently*).
5. **Tier 5 (Lowest):** Social media, anonymous blogs, Reddit. (*Treat only as a starting point for investigation*).

## Identifying Misinformation Patterns

When analyzing a claim or source, actively hunt for these physical markers of manipulation:

### 1. Statistical Manipulation
- **Cherry-picking:** Focusing on a specific 2-year window to show a "trend" that disappears over a 20-year window.
- **Correlation vs. Causation:** E.g., "Ice cream sales cause shark attacks."
- **Misleading Scales:** Bar charts that don't start at zero.

### 2. Context Removal
- **Quote Mining:** Taking a partial sentence perfectly out of context to reverse its meaning.
- **Ignoring Timeframes:** Sharing a disturbing video from 10 years ago as if it happened yesterday.

### 3. Logical Fallacies
- **False Equivalencies:** Treating a fringe conspiracy blog with equal weight to a consensus of 1,000 scientists ("Both-sidesism").
- **Strawman:** Attacking a weakened version of the actual argument.
- **Ad Hominem:** Attacking the person rather than the data.
